# 04 · Monetization

## The principle

**Premium never gates features — only volume.** Every capability is free;
paying buys reach. Free users are full citizens of the corporate world.

## Free (everything)

Resume profiles, behavioral questions, references & endorsements, pipeline
kanban, rejection letters, Open-to-Work badges, Out of Office, all filters
**including Dealbreakers/Minimum Qualifications**, location change
(**Business Trip**), unsend (**Retract Statement**), undo a reject
(**Counteroffer**), **Quarterly Performance Review** analytics, unlimited chat
with matches — and **8 Initial Screens per day**.

## Inbound Applications (the Hinge mechanic)

Everyone has the who-screened-you grid, but for free users it's **blurred** —
you work through your inbound queue one candidate at a time, newest first,
accepting or rejecting each before seeing the next. Premium unblurs the grid
and lets you jump to anyone.

## Premium — "The Executive Suite"

~$14.99/mo (cheaper on 3/6-month commitments):

1. Unlimited Initial Screens per day
2. Unblurred Inbound Applications grid
3. 1 Headhunt/week included

That's it — volume and reach, nothing else.

## Consumables — "Discretionary Budget"

- **Headhunts** (jump someone's queue)
- **Expedited Reviews** (boost — "your resume moves to the top of the stack")

À la carte for anyone, free or paid. Sold via RevenueCat (store IAP rules —
no Stripe on mobile digital goods).

## Post-launch gimmick backlog

**Non-Compete Agreement** (couples mode — both profiles show "Happily
Employed") · **Background Check** (ID/photo verification badge) · **Office
Hours** (availability windows that sync date scheduling) · **Org Chart**
(mutual-friend visibility) · end-of-year **Annual Report**
(Spotify-Wrapped-style share card — viral loop).
